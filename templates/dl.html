About Driving Licence

A Driving Licence is an official document that authorizes an individual to operate motor vehicles on public roads. In India, it is issued by the Regional Transport Office (RTO) and is mandatory for driving any motor vehicle.

The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, governs the issuance of driving licences. There are two main types: Learner's Licence (valid for 6 months) and Permanent Driving Licence (valid for 20 years or until age 50, depending on the issuing authority).

Types of Driving Licence

Learner's Licence

Valid for 6 months. Must be followed by a driving test for permanent licence. Minimum age: 16 years for geared vehicles, 18 years for others.

Permanent Licence

Valid for 20 years or until age 50. Issued after passing the driving test. Requires valid Learner's Licence.

Commercial Licence

For transport vehicles (trucks, buses, taxis). Additional medical and training requirements. Minimum age: 20 years.
