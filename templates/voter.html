About Voter ID Card

The Voter ID card, officially known as the Elector's Photo Identity Card (EPIC), is an identity document issued by the Election Commission of India to adult citizens of India who are eligible to vote. It serves as both a voting credential and a widely accepted proof of identity and address.

The EPIC card was introduced to prevent electoral fraud and impersonation. Any Indian citizen aged 18 years or above is eligible to register as a voter. The card contains the voter's photograph, name, age, address, and unique EPIC number.

Eligibility Criteria: Indian citizen, aged 18 years or above on the qualifying date (January 1 of the year), ordinarily resident of the constituency where applying.

Types of Voter ID Applications

New Registration (Form 6)

For first-time voters who have turned 18 or newly shifted to a constituency. Includes photo and biometric capture.

Correction (Form 8)

For changes in name, address, date of birth, photo, or any other details in existing EPIC card.

Transfer/Migration

For voters shifting from one constituency to another. Requires deletion from previous roll.
